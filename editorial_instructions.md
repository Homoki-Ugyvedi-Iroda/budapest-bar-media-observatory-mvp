# Editorial Instructions — AI Media Observatory Newsletter

## Language and Audience

- The newsletter is written entirely in **Hungarian**
- It is addressed to **lawyers and legal professionals**
- Use clear, professional language appropriate for a legal audience
- Avoid overly technical jargon; briefly explain AI concepts where necessary

## Structure

1. **Header**
   - Title: *AI Figyelő — [hónap, év]* (e.g. *AI Figyelő — 2026. március*)
   - One or two introductory sentences summarising the edition (how many items, from which countries)

2. **News grouped by bar association**
   - Each bar association appears as a section heading, using its full name and country
   - Within each section, individual news items are clearly separated

3. **Per news item**
   - Source link: the bar association's short name as a hyperlink to the original article
   - A summary in Hungarian of **no more than 1000 characters**
   - The summary should capture: what happened or was announced, why it is relevant to lawyers, and any practical implications if apparent.
   - Keep in mind that the newsletter is sent to Hungarian lawyers, while the news items are addressed to local lawyers in that country, so most of the news are just noteworthy for readers of the newsletter.

## Priorities

- Prefer items that directly concern the legal profession (regulation, guidance, tools for lawyers)
- Prefer items about AI policy, ethics, or legislation over general technology news
- If multiple items from the same bar cover similar topics, they may be briefly cross-referenced

## Translation terminology
- Always translate "lawyer" as "ügyvéd" into Hungarian, never into "jogász".
